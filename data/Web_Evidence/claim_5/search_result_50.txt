CLAT 2020 postponed again: New CLAT dates to be announced by September 1
The CLAT 2020 exam scheduled to be held on August 22 has been postponed until further notice.
CLAT 2020: The Consortium of National Law Universities has once again postponed the Common Law Admission Test (CLAT) 2020 examination. The new CLAT 2020 exam dates will be announced after September 1. All the CLAT 2020 aspirants are advised to keep a close check on the official website of CLAT 2020 for the latest updates on fresh examination dates. Earlier, the CLAT 2020 was scheduled to be conducted on August 22.
Originally, CLAT 2020 was first announced to be conducted on May 10, which was later postponed to May 24. It was again postponed to June 21, 2020.
"The exam scheduled for 22nd August 2020 has been postponed until further notice," the official notice read.
As per reports, the decision to postpone the CLAT 2020 was announced on Wednesday by Vice-Chancellor of Dharmashastra National Law University (DNLU), Jabalpur and CLAT 2020 Convenor Professor Balraj Chouhan.
This year, various competitive and recruitment exams have been postponed multiple times due to Covid-19 outbreak and lockdown in various parts of the country.
About CLAT
CLAT is a national level entrance exam for admissions to undergraduate (UG) and postgraduate (PG) law programmes offered by 22 National Law Universities around the country. The CLAT examination is organised by the Consortium of National Law Universities consisting of representative universities.
Registration process
Meanwhile, the online registration process for CLAT 2020 started on January 1, 2020, and the last date to apply for the same was July 10.
Admit card/hall ticket for CLAT 2020
The admit card/hall ticket for CLAT 2020 can be downloaded by the candidate by logging into his/her account. The exact date on which the hall tickets will be made available will be intimated on the website.
CLAT 2020 UG: Exam pattern
The UG-CLAT 2020 shall be a 2-hour test, with 150 multiple-choice questions carrying 1 mark each. There shall be negative marking of 0.25 marks for every wrong answer.
These questions would be divided across the following 5 subjects:
- English Language
- Current Affairs, including General Knowledge
- Legal Reasoning
- Logical Reasoning
- Quantitative Techniques
CLAT 2020 (PG): Exam pattern
The PG-CLAT 2020 will feature an emphasis on the comprehension abilities of the students. It shall be of 120 minutes duration, with one section:
The first section would include 120 objective-type questions carrying 1 mark each. There shall be a negative marking of 0.25 marks for every wrong answer.
The paper will be based on the mandatory subjects of the undergraduate program and include Constitutional Law, Jurisprudence, Administrative Law, Law of Contract, Torts, Family Law, Criminal Law, Property Law, Company Law, Public International Law, Tax Law, Environmental Law, and Labour and Industrial Law.
Candidates are advised to visit CLAT 2020 website regularly for updates.
